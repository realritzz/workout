Lower body · Glute & thigh focus
Monday
Glutes · Quads · Hamstrings · Core
All machine-based — no free-standing balance required. Finish with ankle PT.
🔥 Warmup: 10 min seated bike at easy pace — low impact, warms the legs without stressing the ankle
Exercises
1Leg press
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets10–12 reps60s rest
Feet shoulder width, mid-platform. Don't lock knees at the top. Keep the weight moderate — this is the foundation of your leg work.
2Seated hamstring curl
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets10–12 reps60s rest
Curl all the way through, squeeze at full contraction. Slow on the return — 3 seconds down. Seated version is easier on the ankle than lying.
3Hip thrust (machine or bench)
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets8–12 reps60s rest
Upper back on the bench, feet flat and shoulder width. Drive hips up and squeeze glutes hard at the top — hold 1 second. Keep chin tucked. Best glute exercise there is.
4Hip abduction machine
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets12–15 reps45s rest
Push outward against the pads. Slow and controlled — feel the outer glute squeeze. Don't just throw legs out.
5Reverse crunch
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets10–15 reps45s rest
Lie on a bench or mat. Pull knees toward chest using your core, not momentum. Lower slowly. No wrist load at all — good for carpal tunnel.

Upper body + core
Wednesday
Chest · Back · Shoulders · Core
All machine and cable work — wrist stays in neutral position throughout. Finish with wrist PT.
🔥 Warmup: 10 min seated bike · 10 arm circles · 10 shoulder rolls
Exercises
🖐️ Use neutral grip — keep wrist straight
1Chest press machine
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets8–12 reps60s rest
Handles at mid-chest height. Press straight out. Choose a machine with neutral (palms-facing) handles if available — much easier on the wrists than a pronated grip.
2Seated cable row
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets8–12 reps60s rest
Sit tall, pull handle to belly button. Squeeze shoulder blades at the end. Use a neutral grip V-bar attachment — keeps wrists in a safe position.
3Lat pulldown
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets8–12 reps60s rest
Pull bar to upper chest, lean back slightly. Think about pulling your elbows down to your hips — not pulling with your arms. Use a wider grip only if wrists feel comfortable.
🖐️ Light weight — wrist extension under load
4Lateral raise (dumbbells)
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets12–15 reps45s rest
Go very light here. Lead with your elbows, raise only to shoulder height. Keep the wrist flat and relaxed — don't let the dumbbell bend your wrist back. Stop if wrists ache.
🖐️ Cable tension — keep wrists neutral throughout
5Pallof press (cable)
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets10–12 reps45s rest
Stand side-on to the cable, hold the handle at chest. Press straight out, hold 2 seconds, return. Your core is resisting rotation — don't let the cable pull you. Great anti-rotation core work with minimal wrist stress.
❄️ Cooldown: 5 min walk · Cross-body shoulder stretch · Then: Wrist PT routine

Physical therapy · Daily
PT Routines
Wrist · Ankle · Every day
These take 10–15 min total. Do them daily — they protect your joints and are how you actually get better over time.
🖐️ Wrist PT
Daily · 5 minutes · Carpal tunnel management
1
Tendon glides
Start with hand open, fingers straight. Curl fingers into a hook fist, then a full fist, then a flat fist (fingers bent at knuckle, tips touching palm). Hold each position 3 seconds. × 10 reps.
2
Median nerve glides
Extend arm out, wrist bent back (like a stop signal), fingers spread. Hold 5 seconds. Then tilt your head away from the hand for extra nerve stretch. × 10 reps each hand.
3
Wrist flexor stretch
Extend arm, palm facing up. Use other hand to gently pull fingers back toward you. Hold 30 seconds each hand. Should feel a stretch along the inside of your forearm.
4
Wrist extensor stretch
Extend arm, palm facing down. Use other hand to gently bend your hand downward. Hold 30 seconds each hand. Stretches the top of the forearm — important for typing and lifting.

🦶 Ankle PT
Daily · 5–10 minutes · Strength & mobility recovery
1
Ankle alphabet
Seated, lift one foot slightly. Use your big toe to draw every letter of the alphabet in the air. Full range of motion. Both feet. Builds mobility in all directions.
2
Ankle circles
Slow, full circles — 10 clockwise, 10 counterclockwise. Both ankles. Move through the full range without pain. If it grinds or clicks, go slower.
3
Heel raises
Standing at a wall for support, raise up onto toes, lower slowly. × 15–20 reps. Build calf strength which protects the ankle. Add single-leg over time.
4
Toe raises
Standing, lift toes off the ground while heels stay down. Hold 2 seconds, lower. × 15 reps. Strengthens the shin muscles which stabilize the ankle.
5
Single-leg balance
Stand on one foot, hold for 20–30 seconds. Use a wall nearby for safety. Build up over weeks. This is one of the best things you can do for ankle stability long-term.
📋 Ground rules
🥩
Protein first
0.7–1g per lb of goal bodyweight daily. Eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt. Consistent protein = consistent progress.
⚠️
Listen to your body
Pain is different from discomfort. Muscle burn = good. Sharp joint pain = stop immediately.
😴
Sleep = growth
7–9 hours. Your ankle and wrist also recover during sleep. This isn't optional.
📈
Progressive overload
Add a rep or tiny bit of weight every 1–2 weeks. Slow progress is still progress.
🔁
Consistency wins
Missed a day? Pick right back up. Months of 80% effort beats one perfect week.
💧
Hydration
80–100oz daily. More on gym days. Dehydration worsens joint pain and nerve symptoms.
