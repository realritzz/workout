🪜Stair master
20–30 minSlow controlled pace

Great for glutes and cardio simultaneously. Slow pace protects the ankle and still burns significantly.

Physical therapy · Daily
PT Routines
Wrist · Ankle · Every day
10–15 min total. Do these every day — they protect your joints and are how you actually get better long term.
🖐️Wrist PT
Daily · 5 minutes · Carpal tunnel management
1
Tendon glides
Hand open and flat. Curl into a hook fist, then full fist, then flat fist (fingertips touch palm). Hold each 3 seconds. × 10 reps each hand.
2
Median nerve glides
Arm out, wrist bent back like a stop signal, fingers spread. Hold 5 seconds. Tilt head away from the hand for extra stretch. × 10 reps each hand.
3
Wrist flexor stretch
Arm out palm up, use other hand to gently pull fingers back toward you. Hold 30 seconds each hand. Stretch along the inside of the forearm.
4
Wrist extensor stretch
Arm out palm down, use other hand to gently bend hand downward. Hold 30 seconds each hand. Stretches the top of the forearm — important for typing and lifting.

🦶Ankle PT
Daily · 5–10 minutes · Strength & mobility
1
Ankle alphabet
Seated, lift one foot slightly. Draw every letter with your big toe. Full range of motion. Both feet.
2
Ankle circles
10 slow circles clockwise, 10 counterclockwise. Both ankles. If it grinds or clicks, slow down further.
3
Heel raises
Standing at a wall for support. Rise onto toes, lower slowly. × 15–20 reps. Builds calf strength which stabilizes the ankle. Progress to single-leg over time.
4
Toe raises
Standing, lift toes off the ground, heels stay down. Hold 2 seconds, lower. × 15 reps. Strengthens the shin — critical for ankle stability.
5
Single-leg balance
Stand on one foot, hold 20–30 seconds. Wall nearby for safety. One of the best long-term investments for ankle health.
📋 Ground rules
🥩
Protein first
0.7–1g per lb of goal bodyweight daily. Eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt. Consistent protein = consistent results.
⚠️
Pain vs discomfort
Muscle burn is good. Sharp joint pain means stop immediately — never push through ankle or wrist pain.
😴
Sleep = growth
7–9 hours. Ankle and wrist recover during sleep too. Non-negotiable.
📈
Progressive overload
Add a rep or small amount of weight every 1–2 weeks. Slow progress is still progress.
🔁
Consistency wins
Missed a day? Pick right back up. Months of 80% beats one perfect week every time.
💧
Hydration
80–100oz daily. More on gym days. Dehydration worsens joint pain and nerve symptoms.
