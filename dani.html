🚴 Warmup machine
🚴Seated stationary bike
10 minEasy resistanceWarmup pace
Seat height: when your foot is at the bottom of the pedal stroke, your knee should have a slight bend — not fully locked. Resistance low enough that you can hold a conversation. This is warmup, not cardio — save the effort for the weights.
Exercises
1Leg press
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets10–12 reps60s rest
Feet shoulder width, mid-platform. Don't lock knees at the top. Controlled all the way — don't let the weight crash down.
2Seated hamstring curl
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets10–12 reps60s rest
Curl all the way through, squeeze at full contraction. 3 seconds on the way back up. Seated version is easier on the ankle than lying curl.
3Hip thrust (machine or bench)
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets8–12 reps60s rest
Upper back on bench, feet flat and shoulder width. Drive hips up and squeeze glutes hard at the top — hold 1 second. Keep chin tucked. The best glute exercise there is.
4Hip abduction machine
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets12–15 reps45s rest
Push outward against the pads slowly. Feel the outer glute squeeze. Don't just throw legs out — controlled movement both ways.
5Reverse crunch
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets10–15 reps45s rest
Lie on a bench or mat. Pull knees toward chest using your core, not momentum. Lower slowly. No wrist load — good for carpal tunnel.

Exercises

1Chest press machine
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets8–12 reps60s rest
Handles at mid-chest. Press straight out. Pick the machine with neutral (palms-facing) handles if your gym has one — much kinder to the wrists than a pronated grip.
2Seated cable row
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets8–12 reps60s rest
Use the V-bar attachment (neutral grip). Sit tall, pull to belly button, squeeze shoulder blades together at the end. Don't round your back on the return.
3Lat pulldown
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets8–12 reps60s rest
Pull bar to upper chest, lean back slightly. Think elbows down to your hips — not pulling with your arms. Use a comfortable grip width that doesn't stress the wrists.

4Lateral raise (dumbbells)
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets12–15 reps45s rest
Go very light. Lead with elbows, raise only to shoulder height. Keep the wrist flat and relaxed — don't let the dumbbell bend it back. Stop if wrists ache.
🖐️ Wrists neutral throughout
5Pallof press (cable)
Watch form demo on YouTube ↗
3 sets10–12 reps45s rest
Stand side-on to the cable, hold handle at chest. Press straight out, hold 2 seconds, return. Your core resists the rotation — don't let the cable pull you. Great anti-rotation core work.

🔄 Synced — cardio day for both
Cardio
Saturday
Endurance · Fat burn · Active
Dedicated cardio day. Bike is always safe. Stair master on good ankle days only — never push through ankle pain.
🚴 Option 1 — Seated bike (always safe)
🚴Seated stationary bike
30–40 minModerate resistanceSteady pace
Seat height: knee slightly bent at the bottom of the stroke. Resistance should feel like a brisk effort — you can still talk but wouldn't want to hold a full conversation. This is actual cardio effort, not warmup pace. Build duration and resistance week by week.

Interval option: 2 min easy / 1 min hard × 10 rounds = 30 min. Burns more calories and keeps it mentally engaging.
🪜 Option 2 — Stair master (good ankle days only)
🪜Stair master
20–30 minSlow controlled pace
Step onto the machine, set speed low (level 4–6 to start). Step through the full motion — don't skip steps or bounce. Hands rest lightly on the side rails for balance, not for leaning your weight on them — that cheats the workout and stresses the wrists.

Great for glutes and cardio at the same time. The slow pace isn't weakness — it protects the ankle and still burns significantly.
⚠️ Ankle — good days only. Any pain = bike instead, no exceptions.
💡 Which to pick: If ankle is good, do 20 min stair master then 15 min bike. If ankle is off, do the full 35–40 min on the bike. Either way you hit the cardio goal.

Physical therapy · Daily
PT Routines
Wrist · Ankle · Every day
10–15 min total. Do these every day — they protect your joints and are how you actually get better long term.
🖐️Wrist PT
Daily · 5 minutes · Carpal tunnel management
1
Tendon glides
Hand open and flat. Curl into a hook fist, then full fist, then flat fist (fingertips touch palm). Hold each 3 seconds. × 10 reps each hand.
2
Median nerve glides
Arm out, wrist bent back like a stop signal, fingers spread. Hold 5 seconds. Tilt head away from the hand for an extra stretch. × 10 reps each hand.
3
Wrist flexor stretch
Arm out palm up, use other hand to gently pull fingers back toward you. Hold 30 seconds each hand. Stretch along the inside of the forearm.
4
Wrist extensor stretch
Arm out palm down, use other hand to gently bend hand downward. Hold 30 seconds each hand. Stretches the top of the forearm — important for typing and lifting.

🦶Ankle PT
Daily · 5–10 minutes · Strength & mobility
1
Ankle alphabet
Seated, lift one foot slightly. Draw every letter of the alphabet with your big toe. Full range of motion. Both feet. Builds mobility in all directions.
2
Ankle circles
10 slow circles clockwise, 10 counterclockwise. Both ankles. Full range — if it grinds or clicks, slow down further.
3
Heel raises
Standing at a wall for support. Rise up onto toes, lower slowly. × 15–20 reps. Builds calf strength which stabilizes the ankle. Progress to single-leg over time.
4
Toe raises
Standing, lift toes off the ground, heels stay down. Hold 2 seconds, lower. × 15 reps. Strengthens the shin — critical for ankle stability.
5
Single-leg balance
Stand on one foot, hold 20–30 seconds. Wall nearby for safety. One of the best long-term investments for ankle health. Build up over weeks.
📋 Ground rules
🥩
Protein first
0.7–1g per lb of goal bodyweight daily. Eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt. Consistent protein = consistent results.
⚠️
Pain vs discomfort
Muscle burn is good. Sharp joint pain means stop immediately — never push through ankle or wrist pain.
😴
Sleep = growth
7–9 hours. Ankle and wrist recover during sleep too. Non-negotiable.
📈
Progressive overload
Add a rep or small amount of weight every 1–2 weeks. Slow progress is still progress.
🔁
Consistency wins
Missed a day? Pick right back up. Months of 80% beats one perfect week every time.
💧
Hydration
80–100oz daily. More on gym days. Dehydration worsens joint pain and nerve symptoms.
